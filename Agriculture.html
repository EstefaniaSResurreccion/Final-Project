Agriculture

Our country's agriculture consists of fishery, farming, livestock, and forestry. Just a few years after the global pandemic, the agricultural status of the Philippines rose by 19% in the year 2023. One of the problems that this sector is currently facing is our reliance on imports, especially with rice. However, in recent cases, exports from our country have also flourished.

Import

How did importing rice become a problem? One of the reasons is because we relied on it too much, impacting not only local farmers and their harvest but also the price of these products. Although even if we do lessen our imports, we would also have a significant shortage. According to (BusinessMirror Editorial, 2024), "The latest data from the Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA) attest to the fact that the country remains reliant on foreign farmers for its food supply." This justifies how the Philippines heavily relies on imports as much as or even more than its exports.

Export

In the Export department of our agriculture, it has been observed that in recent cases it rose up to 10.7 percent in value. These include products such as vegetables, fruits, nuts, and many more products. With this news, our country might reach its goal of 2028 as an export powerhouse (PSA, 2024).
